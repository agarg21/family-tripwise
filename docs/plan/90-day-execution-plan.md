# 90-Day Execution Plan

## Goal

Validate whether interactive, family-specific destination pages can rank and convert better than static family travel blog posts.

## Days 1-30: Foundation and First Cluster

Build:

- Brand/domain decision.
- Repository and documentation.
- Content model.
- First 10 pages:
  - San Diego cluster
  - Las Vegas cluster
  - New York City cluster
- First version of where-to-stay recommender.
- Basic activity data structure.

Success metrics:

- 10 high-quality pages published or ready to publish.
- First tool usable for one destination.
- Internal linking and metadata in place.

## Days 31-60: Expand and Add Utility

Build:

- Orlando / Disney World cluster.
- Chicago cluster.
- Miami cluster.
- San Antonio cluster.
- Activity filters.
- First itinerary builder prototype.
- First hotel comparison table.

Success metrics:

- 20-25 pages published or ready.
- 3-5 destinations have structured data.
- Recommender can handle multiple destinations.

## Days 61-90: Scale and Improve SERP Fit

Build:

- Washington DC cluster.
- Paris, France cluster.
- London, UK cluster.
- Rainy-day planner.
- Map export.
- Review summarization workflow.
- Editorial QA checklist.

Success metrics:

- 30+ pages published or ready.
- 10 destination clusters planned.
- 5 destination clusters live or implementation-ready.
- First signs of impressions in Search Console after launch.

## Traffic Targets

These are directional, assuming a new domain:

| Timeframe | Monthly organic sessions |
|---|---:|
| 3 months | 500-2,000 |
| 6 months | 5,000-15,000 |
| 12 months | 25,000-75,000 |

## Key Risks

- Publishing thin AI content.
- Over-scaling before page quality is high.
- Inaccurate hotel or safety advice.
- Stale attraction, transit, and hotel data.
- Not having enough real-world trust signals.

## Mitigations

- Keep human review in the loop.
- Start with fewer destinations and deeper utility.
- Store source dates for recommendations.
- Mark inferred scores clearly.
- Add tools that make the pages meaningfully better than static SERPs.
