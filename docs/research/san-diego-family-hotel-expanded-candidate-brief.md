Status: internal planning research; not publication-ready

## Decision

The first San Diego family-hotel page should not be limited to the three hotels already named on the current where-to-stay page. Those three were useful because they proved the evidence workflow on existing claims, but a standalone hotel-intent page needs enough breadth to answer the searcher without feeling thin.

Use the eight primary candidates covered by `FT-RES-005` as the first full evidence set, then keep additional hotels in a watchlist. The public page should still feel clean: category picks and a compact comparison surface first, with evidence drawers only for parents who want the detail.

## Price Standard

Do not require a full booking-screen capture before a hotel can enter the research shortlist.

For the first publishable page, use a visible `approx nightly room band` instead of a booking promise:

| Band | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `$` | Usually under $250 before final taxes, fees, and room upgrades. |
| `$$` | Often $250-$400 before final taxes, fees, and room upgrades. |
| `$$$` | Often $400-$650 before final taxes, fees, and room upgrades. |
| `$$$$` | Often above $650, or highly variable by room category/season. |

Rules:

- Use only as a planning clue, not a quoted rate.
- Record the source, date, party/date context if visible, and whether taxes/mandatory fees appear included.
- Prefer official `starting from` or direct-booking visibility where available; otherwise use reputable public OTA/metasearch snapshots as volatile evidence.
- Keep parking, resort/destination fees, and breakfast separate from the band because they change family value quickly.
- Recheck bands within 14 days of publication and every 30 days while the page is live.

## Review-Signal Standard

Recent family-relevant reviews should be part of the public evidence record. The target page should not copy review text or overstate a handful of anecdotes.

For every primary candidate, collect:

- at least two public review sources where feasible;
- visible date range, preferably 2025-2026 for the first pass;
- a sample-size bucket such as `5-10 visible reviews`, `10-25 visible reviews`, or `25+ visible reviews`;
- family or kid-specific signals when visible, including pool use, breakfast friction, room setup, noise, walking/parking, and service recovery;
- repeated positives, repeated negatives, conflicts, and the parent check triggered by each conflict.

Community/forum signals stay separate from verified-stay review signals. Reddit can tell us what parents worry about and which hotels get named in real planning conversations, but one thread should not make a hotel a winner.

## Amenity Detail Standard

Crib, rollaway, connecting-room, and kitchen details are useful but should not be mandatory for every hotel record.

Use this priority:

1. Always capture room setup, family occupancy, suite/kitchen/kitchenette, parking, resort/destination fees, pool/water features, breakfast inclusion, and location/friction notes when visible.
2. Capture crib, rollaway, connecting-room, laundry, microwave, mini-fridge, kids club, and shuttle details when they are easy to find from official sources.
3. If a detail is not visible, mark it `not found in inspected official sources` instead of blocking the whole hotel.
4. Require a parent-facing `verify before booking` line for any missing room, bedding, crib, rollaway, connecting-room, or cancellation detail.

## Page Implication

The first live page should show no more than 6-8 primary hotel cards. It can mention the watchlist briefly, but should not become a cluttered directory.

Use category labels instead of an ordinal ranking. Good labels:

- best researched fit for pool-first Mission Bay trips;
- best researched fit for LEGOLAND-heavy trips;
- best researched fit for suite/kitchen downtown trips;
- best researched fit for stay-put bay resort trips;
- best researched fit for Coronado splurge trips.

Avoid labels like `best hotel in San Diego`, `safest`, `quietest`, `most stroller-friendly`, or `guaranteed best for families` unless a later evidence record and any required human review can support them.
