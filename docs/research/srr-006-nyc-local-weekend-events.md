## User Context

The user lives in Jersey City and would personally find a local/nearby-parent planner useful for free or paid events, things to do, and experiences around New York City, Jersey City, Hoboken, and nearby reachable areas.

Examples the user mentioned:

- [JCFamilies Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/jcfamilies/?hl=en)

Instagram accounts should be treated as competitor/source-discovery signals only. Do not scrape private or login-only Instagram content, and do not rely on Instagram as the durable primary source for event details unless the same details are publicly visible and source-linked.

## Bottom Line

This should not start as a pure SEO article. The useful product is a hybrid:

- an indexable SEO landing page once reviewed content exists;
- a weekly, manually reviewed event/activity table;
- filters for free/paid, indoor/rainy-day, age band, area, transit from Jersey City/Hoboken, reservation need, and source freshness;
- a future newsletter/module path if the curation workflow proves sustainable.

The core opportunity is not "another list of NYC events." Existing competitors already have broad calendars. Family Tripwise can create original value by serving nearby parents who need a cross-Hudson, logistics-aware decision aid.

## Scope Decision

Recommended initial scope: greater NYC metro families, with a practical anchor in Jersey City/Hoboken plus easy NYC access.

Do not start NYC-only:

- NYC-only has the largest likely search surface, but incumbents are strong and the page would be less differentiated.
- It would underserve the user's concrete Jersey City/Hoboken use case.

Do not start Jersey City/Hoboken-only:

- It is likely more personally useful, but SEO demand is probably smaller and source coverage is narrower.
- Many Jersey City/Hoboken families actively consider Manhattan, Brooklyn waterfront, and transit-reachable activities.

Recommended framing:

- Use the NYC slug for search discovery: `/things-to-do/nyc-this-weekend-with-kids.html`.
- Make the page title and intro explicit that the planner covers NYC, Jersey City, Hoboken, and nearby reachable areas.
- Treat "Jersey City/Hoboken", "Manhattan", "Brooklyn waterfront", and "nearby NJ" as first-class filters.

## Why This Can Be Original

Competitors are strong, but their weakness is parent-specific decision support across city/state boundaries.

Family Tripwise can add value through:

- source timestamps and last-checked dates;
- free/paid filter;
- indoor/rainy-day flag;
- age-band fit;
- reservation/ticket requirement;
- stroller/transit notes clearly labeled as estimated or human-reviewed;
- "from Jersey City/Hoboken" logistics, including PATH/ferry/subway/rideshare/car context;
- commute-friction bands rather than generic neighborhood names;
- grouping by family state: toddler morning, school-age outing, teen-friendly, mixed-age, low-cost, rainy day, heat/cold backup;
- a weekly review workflow that prevents stale or unverified event details from going live.

## Publishing And Freshness Guardrails

Do not automatically publish event data yet.

Required workflow before publishing:

1. Gather candidates from source tiers.
2. Verify each candidate against a public source URL.
3. Record last-checked timestamp.
4. Mark logistics notes as estimated unless human-reviewed.
5. Exclude or expire past events automatically in the build or manually before publication.
6. Human reviews the final weekly table before it is published.

Source tiers:

- Tier 1: official venue, city/county, library, park, museum, cultural institution, transit/transportation, or event organizer pages.
- Tier 2: established local editorial calendars such as Mommy Poppins, Brooklyn Bridge Parents, JCFamilies, Hoboken Girl, NJ Mom, NJ Kids, Macaroni KID.
- Tier 3: ticketing/event platforms such as Eventbrite.
- Tier 4: Instagram/social posts, used only as discovery unless public and source-linked.

No event should be published from Tier 4 alone.
