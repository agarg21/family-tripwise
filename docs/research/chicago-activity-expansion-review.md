## Competitive usefulness review

Current ranking-page patterns tend to fall into four groups:

1. Local and family publishers provide very broad lists and city familiarity, but lists can create high scan cost and weak prioritization.
2. Official tourism and venue pages have the freshest operating facts, but do not make the cross-venue family decision.
3. Major travel publishers add authority and editorial selection, but often offer limited stroller, nap, weather, and route support.
4. Community discussions are useful for honest pacing questions, especially whether two large museums fit one day, but are inconsistent and anecdotal.

Family Tripwise does not need a novel attraction list to be useful. The opportunity is a cleaner comparison: enough breadth to prevent a false shortlist, then fewer route starts, explicit stop rules, and direct current-source checks. The page should beat weak results on decision speed and maintenance quality, not claim local or firsthand authority it does not have.
