# Las Vegas Family-Hotel Evidence Pack

Status: publication evidence for `FT-PUB-002`

Checked: 2026-07-22

Market: US family travel

## Decision

Publish one named-property comparison at `https://familytripwise.com/where-to-stay/las-vegas-family-hotels.html` covering ten hotels with meaningfully different trip shapes. Keep `where-to-stay/las-vegas-with-kids.html` focused on area and property constraints, and route readers between the two pages. Do not create a separate family-resorts URL.

The page is a comparison, not an ordinal ranking. It can identify a useful starting point for a trip style, but it cannot claim a universal best hotel, a personally verified stay, a safe or quiet room, an exact stroller route, guaranteed pool access, or stable value.

## Why This Page

- Fresh Semrush US evidence collected July 22 reports 1,900 monthly searches for `family friendly hotels in Las Vegas`, 880 for `best family hotels in Las Vegas`, 720 for `Las Vegas family resorts`, and 480 for `kid-friendly hotels in Las Vegas`. These variants overlap and must not be added together.
- Observed July 22 SERPs repeatedly return named-property lists for all four variants. That is a different job from the existing area/constraint page.
- Current ranking pages are broad but uneven. OTAs have inventory and review scale; major publishers have editorial or firsthand authority. Many pages still make it hard to compare total-price posture, room function, casino/non-gaming context, pool seasonality, resort walking, and the exact family check that could invalidate a choice.
- Family Tripwise can compete on a compact decision surface, current official facts, rough total-night planning ranges, and bounded review conflicts. It should not imply local or firsthand authority it does not have.

## Evidence Method

| Evidence | Use in this pack | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| `OFFICIAL_PROPERTY_FACT` | Current room capacity, kitchens, pools, attractions, parking posture, and property identity. | Does not prove enjoyment, reliability, quiet, cleanliness, or value. |
| `PRICE_BAND_EVIDENCE` | Broad USD total-night planning range from public direct/OTA/metasearch examples checked July 22. | Not a quote or promise; dates, occupancy, room type, taxes, fees, parking, and events change it. |
| `REVIEW_SIGNAL` | Paraphrased themes and conflicts from a small or thin sample of recent public reviews, prioritizing family-tagged entries where visible. | Not a representative survey, aggregate score, or firsthand verification. |
| `COMMUNITY_SIGNAL` | Parent question discovery and contradiction prompts from public discussions. | Anecdotal only; never a property fact or demand metric. |
| `EDITORIAL_INTERPRETATION` | A bounded trip-style starting point tied to the evidence below. | Not a universal ranking or firm suitability guarantee. |

Price method: ranges synthesize public examples visible in the next-30-day context and broader visible rate examples rather than completing a personal booking flow. They are intentionally wide and include mandatory taxes/fees where the public display said they were included. Parking and optional purchases may still sit outside a displayed total. Recheck within 14 days of a material hotel-page update.

Review method: each selected hotel has at least one public booking/review source. Samples are `small` (roughly 3-10 recent visible entries/snippets) unless marked `thin` (roughly 2-4). Review prose and reviewer identifiers are not retained. Recheck within 60 days or sooner after a renovation, closure, rebrand, or strong conflict.

## Page Architecture

1. Headline and two-sentence scope.
2. Compact trust strip with checked date and price meaning.
3. Five trip-style starting points.
4. One ten-row comparison table.
5. Ten hotel cards, each limited to official fit, room/family setup, sampled online-review themes, rough price context, and one verification check.
6. Direct Google Maps link per hotel; no external My Maps account mutation or heavy embedded map.
7. Visible methodology and source list.
8. Visible FAQ aligned with schema.

The page should not expose internal evidence labels such as `REVIEW_SIGNAL`, display a defensive disclaimer block, or repeat the same caveat in every field. Uncertainty belongs in plain-language checks.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Exactly one new canonical/indexable URL, present once in the sitemap.
- Ten named hotels and ten direct Google Maps links.
- Every hotel has an official source, rough total-night range, recent public-review source, conflict, freshness date, and parent check.
- No ordinal ranking, affiliate/booking CTA, copied review prose, reviewer identifiers, firsthand claim, safety/quiet/route guarantee, or indexing request.
- Existing Las Vegas stay page and home page link to the comparison; the newly released activity page is unchanged.
- ItemList and FAQ schema match visible content.
- Generator regeneration is idempotent and changes only the expected site files.
- Native QA, focused tests, responsive browser checks, and independent review pass before release.

## Measurement Plan
