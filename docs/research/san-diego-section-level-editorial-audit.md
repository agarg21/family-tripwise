## Decision Standard

Cross-page overlap is acceptable when each page gives useful detail for its own planning job. This audit therefore focuses on whether every visible section earns its place within the page. Each section is classified as keep, compress, merge, move/link, remove, or verify.

Evidence classification:

- page copy and structure: direct repository inspection on July 20, 2026;
- persona and intent: reused current `docs/research/san-diego-cluster-research-decision-pack.md` evidence;
- ranking-page context: reused current, directional SERP evidence from that decision pack and `docs/research/san-diego-family-stay-competitor-research.md`;
- GSC: fresh authenticated read-only snapshot collected July 20 with finalized data through July 18, used only for page/index context;
- no new firsthand, route, safety, access, or family-suitability evidence was created by this editorial audit.

## Main Finding

The San Diego pages contain useful comparison and parent-logistics material. The main editorial weakness is within-page repetition: the same small set of choices can appear as starter cards, quick picks, a comparison table, detailed cards, and mini-itineraries. The fix is not to remove detail. It is to keep one scan layer, one detail layer, and one application layer.
