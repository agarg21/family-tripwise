# Chicago Family-Hotel Evidence Pack

Status: publication evidence for `FT-PUB-003`

Checked: 2026-07-23

Market: US family travel

## Decision

Publish one named-property comparison at `https://familytripwise.com/where-to-stay/chicago-family-hotels.html` covering ten hotels with meaningfully different trip shapes. Keep `where-to-stay/chicago-with-kids.html` focused on the area/base decision and route readers between the two pages. Do not create separate downtown, pool, suite, near-Shedd, Navy Pier, or luxury hotel URLs.

The page is a comparison, not an ordinal ranking. It can identify a useful starting point for a trip style, but it cannot claim a universal best hotel, a personally verified stay, a safe or quiet room, an exact walking or stroller route, guaranteed room connection, stable value, or universal family fit.

## Why This Page

- Logged-in Semrush US evidence collected July 23 reports 320 monthly searches for `best hotels in Chicago for families`, 260 for `family friendly hotels in Chicago`, and 210 for `kid friendly hotels Chicago`. These variants overlap and must not be added together.
- Related constraints also have directional demand: 260 for `Chicago kid-friendly hotels with indoor pool`, 210 for `family friendly hotels downtown Chicago`, and 210 for `Chicago hotels near Shedd Aquarium`. They reuse the same named-property inventory and do not justify separate pages.
- The observed July 23 hotel-list SERPs repeatedly return OTAs, editorial shortlists, property pages, and parent discussions. This differs from the neighborhood and area-guide result set for `where to stay`.
- Current ranking pages have authority, inventory, or local voice, but make it difficult to compare room function, breakfast or kitchen utility, pool status, attraction base, broad total-price posture, and contradictory recent review themes in one calm surface.
- Family Tripwise can compete on structured decisions, current official facts, explicit tradeoffs, and source-dated review conflicts. It should not imitate OTA inventory or imply local, advisor, or firsthand authority it does not have.

## Evidence Method

| Evidence | Use in this pack | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| `OFFICIAL_PROPERTY_FACT` | Current room, suite, kitchen, breakfast, pool, parking, crib, connecting-room, and property-location facts. | Does not prove enjoyment, reliability, cleanliness, quiet, value, or that a request will be honored. |
| `PRICE_BAND_EVIDENCE` | Broad USD total-night planning range from public direct and OTA examples checked July 23. | Not a quote or promise; date, occupancy, room type, taxes, fees, parking, and events change it. |
| `REVIEW_SIGNAL` | Paraphrased observations and conflicts from the bounded, dated public samples logged below, prioritizing family context where visible. | Not a representative survey, aggregate score, or firsthand verification; absence from a sample is not proof of absence. |
| `COMMUNITY_SIGNAL` | Parent-question discovery and contradiction prompts from public Chicago discussions. | Anecdotal only; never a property fact or demand metric. |
| `EDITORIAL_INTERPRETATION` | A bounded trip-style starting point tied to the evidence below. | Not an ordinal ranking or firm suitability guarantee. |

Price method: ranges use visible next-30-day summer 2026 examples for two adults where the public source exposed them, then widen for ordinary date and room-type variation. The cited low examples included taxes and mandatory fees where stated; parking remains outside the nightly range. Recheck within 14 days of a material hotel-page update.

Review method: the primary sample is the latest bounded set of visible, verified Expedia entries inspected July 23, supplemented by named Booking.com or Tripadvisor pages when a conflict or family context needed a second check. The ledger records the exact number inspected, visible date span, whether family context was explicit, and source-to-theme mapping. The live page says only what appeared in the inspected sample; it does not call the themes representative or infer family context when none was exposed. No review prose, reviewer identifier, aggregate score, or raw export is retained. Recheck within 60 days or sooner after a closure, renovation, rebrand, material policy change, or strong new conflict.

## Page Architecture

1. Headline and two-sentence scope.
2. Compact checked-date and price-meaning strip.
3. Five trip-style starting points.
4. One ten-row comparison table.
5. Ten hotel cards limited to official fit, room or family setup, sampled online-review themes, broad price context, and one verification check.
6. Direct Google Maps link per hotel; no heavy embedded map or external My Maps mutation.
7. Visible methodology and source list.
8. Three visible FAQs aligned with schema.

The page should not expose internal labels such as `REVIEW_SIGNAL`, show a defensive disclaimer block, or repeat the same caveat in every field. Uncertainty belongs in the checked date, plain-language conflicts, and each decisive parent check.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Exactly one new canonical and indexable URL, present once in the sitemap.
- Ten named hotels and ten direct Google Maps links.
- Every hotel has an official source, broad total-night range, public review source, conflict, freshness date, and parent check.
- The current Loews pool closure and InterContinental August closure are not obscured.
- No ordinal ranking, affiliate or booking CTA, copied review prose, reviewer identifier, aggregate score, firsthand claim, safety or quiet guarantee, exact route claim, price guarantee, or indexing request.
- Existing Chicago stay and home pages link to the comparison; the released activities page remains unchanged.
- ItemList and FAQ schema match visible content.
- Generator regeneration is idempotent and changes only expected site files.
- Native QA, focused tests, responsive browser checks, and independent review pass before release.
