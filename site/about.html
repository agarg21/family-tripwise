Methodology

How Family Tripwise builds family travel guides

Family travel advice gets useful when it stops treating every family like the same group of adults with smaller shoes. Our guides focus on age fit, pace, stroller friction, weather backup, nap timing, lodging base, and the small logistics that decide whether a day works.

Last updated: July 11, 2026

Current scope: Family Tripwise is running a focused 5-city test before expanding. Current destination clusters are San Diego, Las Vegas, New York City, Chicago, and San Antonio. We update pages as search data, official sources, product needs, and human review notes improve.

Editorial promise

Planning guidance, not fake certainty

Research-based by default

Most Family Tripwise destination pages are built from official attraction pages, official hotel pages, destination information, search-intent research, and structured family planning assumptions. Unless a page says otherwise, it should not be read as a firsthand trip diary.

Human review required

Hotel recommendations, area recommendations, safety notes, stroller/transit advice, and family-suitability claims can materially affect a trip. Those claims must stay caveated until a human reviewer verifies the exact detail or supplies firsthand experience.

Model-derived notes are labeled

When we use model-derived planning judgment, we frame it as guidance to verify. Examples include route friction, nap risk, family fit, hotel tradeoffs, and what to skip on a short trip.

Official sources still win

Hours, ticket rules, parking, fees, pool access, room policies, stroller access, elevator status, weather closures, and event schedules can change. Families should check official sources before booking or promising a plan to kids.

What we evaluate

Family logistics come first

Age fit

We separate toddler, elementary, tween, and teen fit because the same attraction can be perfect for one kid and exhausting for another.

Stroller friction

We flag hills, sand, stairs, crowds, long indoor corridors, subway elevators, and walking routes that can turn a simple outing into a project.

Nap and reset value

We favor plans with realistic morning anchors, easy meals, hotel resets, and optional afternoon stops instead of overstuffed sightseeing days.

Weather backup

We look for indoor or mixed-weather alternatives that can be swapped in without rebuilding the entire day.

Lodging base

For stay pages, we start with area tradeoffs before hotel names: noise, walkability, parking, transit, pool value, room size, and nearby meals.

Pairing nearby

We group attractions that make sense together so families avoid crossing a city twice with tired kids.

Source discipline

What we check before publishing

Each upgraded guide includes official source links for attraction planning, hotel policies, ticketing, parking, accessibility, and photo licensing where relevant. We use those sources to shape planning notes, then keep change-sensitive claims cautious.

For hotels, we separate area guidance from property checks. A hotel may appear as a research candidate because its official page lists a room type, pool, kitchenette, breakfast, parking policy, or location advantage, but that is not the same as a human-reviewed recommendation. Families should verify the exact booking screen, fees, cancellation terms, and room assignment rules before paying.

How pages improve

Search data is input, not the product

GSC signal

Google Search Console helps identify which existing pages Google is testing, such as family stay intent or things-to-do intent. We use that signal to improve decision support on current pages before adding new clusters.

Cluster completeness

A strong destination cluster should connect where-to-stay, things-to-do, itinerary, toddler, teen, and methodology pages so a parent can move from research to a realistic route without opening unrelated listicles.

Useful structure

We prefer quick picks, comparison tables, verification checklists, source notes, and skip guidance over long narrative copy. The page should make tradeoffs visible under time pressure.

No scaled filler

We do not add new city pages just because a keyword exists. Existing pages should earn indexing through parent-specific planning value, clear caveats, and internal links that match the trip-planning workflow.

Limits

What our guides are not

Not a guarantee

Opening hours, resort fees, pool rules, parking costs, and transit conditions can change quickly. Always verify the details that matter for your trip before paying.

Not medical or safety advice

We can flag planning friction, but families should use official advisories and their own judgment for health, safety, mobility, and accessibility needs.

Not a personal trip diary

When a page is research-based rather than firsthand, we keep the language practical and source-backed instead of pretending we personally tested every stop.

Not a hotel booking guarantee

Room size, fees, deposits, parking, rollaways, cribs, kitchenettes, breakfast, pool access, and noise exposure can vary by property, date, room type, and booking channel. Treat our hotel notes as verification prompts.

Not real-time operations data

Transit disruptions, weather, closures, timed-entry availability, and event schedules can change after a guide is updated. The final check should happen close to the trip date.

Not a replacement for parent judgment

Families know their own child energy, sensory needs, nap tolerance, food constraints, mobility needs, and budget. Our job is to make tradeoffs easier to see, not to overrule those constraints.
