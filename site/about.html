Methodology

How Family Tripwise builds family travel guides

Family travel advice gets useful when it stops treating every family like the same group of adults with smaller shoes. Our guides focus on age fit, pace, stroller friction, weather backup, nap timing, lodging base, and the small logistics that decide whether a day works.

Last updated: July 5, 2026

Current scope: Family Tripwise is running a focused 5-city test before expanding. We update pages as search data, official sources, and firsthand notes improve.

What we evaluate

Family logistics come first

Age fit

We separate toddler, elementary, tween, and teen fit because the same attraction can be perfect for one kid and exhausting for another.

Stroller friction

We flag hills, sand, stairs, crowds, long indoor corridors, subway elevators, and walking routes that can turn a simple outing into a project.

Nap and reset value

We favor plans with realistic morning anchors, easy meals, hotel resets, and optional afternoon stops instead of overstuffed sightseeing days.

Weather backup

We look for indoor or mixed-weather alternatives that can be swapped in without rebuilding the entire day.

Lodging base

For stay pages, we start with area tradeoffs before hotel names: noise, walkability, parking, transit, pool value, room size, and nearby meals.

Pairing nearby

We group attractions that make sense together so families avoid crossing a city twice with tired kids.

Source discipline

What we check before publishing

Each upgraded guide includes official source links for attraction planning, hotel policies, ticketing, parking, accessibility, and photo licensing where relevant. We use those sources to shape planning notes, then keep change-sensitive claims cautious.

Official sources
Attractions, hotels, parks, transit, and destination organizations.
Search input
SEO research helps choose pages, but does not replace useful planning detail.
Images
Licensed or public-domain images only; no generated travel photos.
Update checks
Hotel fees, hours, policies, and closures should be verified before booking.

Limits

What our guides are not

Not a guarantee

Opening hours, resort fees, pool rules, parking costs, and transit conditions can change quickly. Always verify the details that matter for your trip before paying.

Not medical or safety advice

We can flag planning friction, but families should use official advisories and their own judgment for health, safety, mobility, and accessibility needs.

Not a personal trip diary

When a page is research-based rather than firsthand, we keep the language practical and source-backed instead of pretending we personally tested every stop.
